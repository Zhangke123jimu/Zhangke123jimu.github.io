This project evaluated clinical outcomes and medical costs of robot-assisted surgery using regional real-world inpatient data from over 80 tertiary or secondary hospitals in Tianjin, 
covering **8,167 patients**, **over 10,000 hospital admissions**, and **millions of structured diagnosis, procedure, prescription, laboratory-test**  across four surgical fields.

As a core researcher, my work focused on large-scale data extraction, cleaning, cohort construction, outcome definition, cost decomposition, and statistical analysis. 
I used SQL and R to process multi-source medical records and applied descriptive analysis and inverse probability of treatment weighting (IPTW) to compare robot-assisted, laparoscopic, and open surgeries under adjusted baseline characteristics.

The project supported health technology assessment and pricing-policy evaluation of laparoscopic surgical robots. Key outcomes included 30- and 90-day postoperative complications, blood transfusion, readmission, length of stay, total medical costs, and cost components.
